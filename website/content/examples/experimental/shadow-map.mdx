## What to inspect

- The thin yellow fence stresses map resolution and PCSS filtering; low quality should lose more of
  the individual slat detail.
- The tall colored blocks cast long shadows whose penumbrae widen across the neutral receiver.
- The receding stair lane crosses fitted cascade ranges. Select **Cascades** to see the active
  light-space view and its blend region.
- The small blocks near the camera make shadow attachment and receiver bias easier to judge.

Use **Visibility** to remove material lighting and inspect only the directional shadow factor.
**Sun Angular Radius** controls the apparent light size, so increasing it makes the PCSS penumbrae
wider. Drag the canvas to orbit manually and use the wheel or trackpad to zoom; disable **Auto
Orbit** when comparing presets from a fixed angle.

## Quality presets

The example passes the selected preset directly to `ShadowMapRenderer`:

| Preset | Directional map | Cascades | Blocker / filter samples |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Low | 1024 | 3 | 8 / 12 |
| Balanced | 1536 | 4 | 16 / 24 |
| Cinematic | 2048 | 4 | 24 / 48 |

The geometry and default light stay fixed while presets change. Pause **Auto Orbit** before a
side-by-side comparison so camera movement does not change cascade fitting or texel alignment.
